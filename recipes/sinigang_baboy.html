Sinigang na Baboy sa Sampalok Recipe

Description

Sinigang is one of the most pupular local filipino dishes that is a staple in most
filipino households. It is a meat and veggie stew that can be made sour in different ways.
This recipe uses pork with sampalok as the souring agent.

Ingredients

  • 1 kg pork cubed
  • 2 tbsp oil
  • 2 pcs onion quartered
  • 3 pcs tomato quartered
  • 3 clove garlic pounded
  • 1.5 L water
  • 3 tbsp fish sauce
  • 2 pcs pork broth cubes
  • 0.5 kg tamarind
  • 500 ml water
  • 3 pcs finger chillies
  • 8 pcs okra halved
  • 1 raddish sliced
  • 2 bunch kangkong

Directions

  1. Heat 2 tbsp of oil in a pot.
  2. Brown pork pieces on high and set aside. ABout two minutes per side.
  3. Saute onions until separated. Add tomatoes and garlic. Saute for 1 minute.
  4. Add the pork, broth cubes, fish sauce, and 1.5 L water. Let boil then simmer for 30 minutes.
  5. To make the tamarind extract, boil 0.5 L water on a smaller pot then add the tamarind.
  6. After 5 minutes, scoop and smash the tamarind pieces using a spoon and strainer.
  7. Return the tamarind in the pot and let boil for 3 more minutes.
  8. Strain the tamarind pieces from the tamarind extract. Set aside tamarind extract.
  9. After the pork has simmered for 30 minutes, add the finger chillies and tamarind extract.
  10. Simmer for 5 minutes then add okra and raddish.
  11. After 8 minutes, check for seasoning. Add more fish sauce to taste.
  12. Add the kangkong cover. Turn off the heat and let he residual heat steam the kangkong for about 2 minutes.
  13. Serve with rice and enjoy!
